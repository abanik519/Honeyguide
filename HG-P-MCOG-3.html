The Honeyguide's Revenge
A story begins with the exposition - this is the part that introduces the characters and the setting and gets the story started.
Then the plot gets going with rising actions - these are the events that lead up to the climax. The rising actions usually involve at least one problem that must be solved.
The climax is the point of major tension in the story.
After the climax there are falling actions. These are plot events that lead toward the resolution of the problem or the conflict.
Finally there is the resolution of the problem.
Today when you read the story for the last time you can think about where the events in the story fit into this story arc.Done
